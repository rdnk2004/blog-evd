The Departure of a Soul…

As the Bhagavad Gita says, there is no death for the soul… the soul may take another attire, not the form we knew.

That was the first time she had truly witnessed a soul departing in such a manner..as she had heard people speak of, read in scriptures, or seen on screens. She had known this person since her childhood, a frequent visitor to her neighbour’s home. As life would have it, she later became a part of his family. But her understanding of him changed gradually as he moved into old age.

Like every human being, he had his own flaws and values. In her childhood, she saw him with a sense of fear. Being the elder of a large family, he was dominant, short-tempered, and people around him treated him with due respect. That was her perception of him at that age.

Years later, when she realized that the next phase of her life would be within his family, the fear dissolved. In its place grew respect and an affection for a new relationship. The family included his wife, sons, a daughter, a son-in-law, and granddaughters. He was no longer the same person. She began to see his softer side within the family as age shaped him, though he remained adamant and self-confident.

Like any family, there were differences of opinion among them, moments of disagreement, but he always stood firm in his views. She was happy to be one among them, for the bond they shared with each other. She hardly had any detailed conversations with him, other than the usual gentle formalities of family life.

Yet, she knew him in her own way. She had deep respect for his knowledge of temples, their histories, rituals, slokas, hymns, literature, and almost everything. At times, she would look through the old scriptures on his shelf. He also had a heart that appreciated goodness in anyone, even in a small child.
She expressed her affection in her own way..by cooking dishes for him whenever she visited. 
As his health began to deteriorate, he faced each phase with strong willpower and courage.
Though miles apart, she always held a prayer for him. Along with it, she carried a quiet wish—that she might be fortunate enough to be there at his feet in his final moments. There was no certainty in it, no expectation…just a wish.
She was unable to visit for months due to circumstances. Later, she got a chance to see him when he was hospitalized, just a few days before that day.

Then, he was home.
Bedridden, with physical discomforts, yet his face carried a sense of contentment. It was his final few hours. Around him stood everyone—his wife, daughter, granddaughter, sons, maid, and nurse….each holding their own emotions.

And at his feet…


She stood.
With prayers for a peaceful departure of the soul.


Looking back at those few hours, she felt it was something beyond coincidence. The universe had unfolded every moment and circumstance for the fulfilment of her silent wish. While the people around him were wrapped in grief, she felt something else..a quiet sense of grace. Only she knew what that moment meant to her.
From her understanding, she realized that being present when a soul chooses to leave is a blessing.
There was also a quiet realization, that he would continue to be a guiding force in her life. It is our beliefs and the way we see things that keep us connected. 
She felt blessed to sense his presence…and she believed in it.

According to Hindu belief, ancestors return to their homes in the form of birds, especially as a crow. On the sixteenth day, after all the rituals were completed and people had begun to leave, the house fell into a deep silence. And there, on the gate facing the home, sat a crow. Hearing its cawing sound, she paused. She was there in that moment, perhaps the only one who noticed…and in that quiet instant, her belief felt alive.

On his first anniversary, the family gathered once again. As tradition followed, food was first offered outside in his remembrance before everyone began eating inside. While serving, she stepped out for a moment—and there it was again. A crow, flying away with a piece of pappad from the food that had been offered.
In that sight, she felt an unexpected happiness to witness his presence.


And…….


Who was that person to her…



Her father-in-law.
